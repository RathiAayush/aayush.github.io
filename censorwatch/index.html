What does the app do?

Web censorship in India can be confusing. We want to understand what websites are blocked by Indian internet service providers (ISPs) and what methods they are employing to block these websites. To do so, the app attempts to establish connections with a set of websites that are possibly blocked in India. To delve into technical details about the network tests, please visit the research page.

How can you help?

You can download the app (currently available only for Android devices) from the Google Play Store, or as a direct download. By running the app, you will be helping us determine what websites your ISP is blocking. Our preliminary research found that all ISPs are not blocking the same websites, and that certain websites were only blocked by a single ISP. If a larger scale study like this confirms that finding, we will have concrete empirical evidence of the fact that ISPs in India are not complying with net neutrality regulations in India.

What information does the app collect?

The app does not ask for any special permissions, and does not collect any personally identifiable information. We only store a device identifier that the app will generate when you start the network tests. Please read the privacy policy for more details.

Who are we?

This app was developed by the Centre for Internet and Society. The Centre for Internet and Society (CIS) is a non-profit organisation that undertakes interdisciplinary research on internet and digital technologies from policy and academic perspectives. Through its diverse initiatives, CIS explores, intervenes in, and advances contemporary discourse and regulatory practices around internet, technology, and society in India, and elsewhere.
